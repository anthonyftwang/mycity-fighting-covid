How to help

The best way for us to help tackle this global challenge is on a local level. Your community's fight may look very different from another's. For that reason there aren't many one-size-fits-all solutions. However, you can easily find out (or may already know) exactly what your area needs! That's why we're open-sourcing code for a modular website design that you can adapt as you see fit for your local reponse.

We also know that coordination and visibility is key, as is learning from the responses in other places. So, we're assembling a collection of local response sites from around the world, making them easy to find and learn from no matter where you are on Earth. Get started and we'll add yours at [mycity].fightingcovid.net.

Determine needs in your community

Reach out to your local health service and community organizations to find out the specific challenges your region is facing. For example, do you have...

  • A shortage of Personal Protective Equipment?
  • Initiatives looking for volunteers?
  • Vulnerable members of the community in need?

Figure out how people can help!

Deploy a website to address your region's needs

Our template is modular, simple, and free to use. If you've already built a site or want to use your own template, that's cool too! Our goal is to make it as easy as possible for anyone to do this. In fact, to host your site all you need is a free GitHub account (via GitHub Pages)! Find detailed steps here, but in short:

  1. Clone the GitHub repository.
  2. Modify the template as much or as little as you need to.
  3. Publish the site using GitHub Pages.
  4. Contact us; we'll verify your site and add it to this domain.
